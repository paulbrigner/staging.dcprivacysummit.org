Keynote Speakers

Hear from the policy leaders, researchers, and builders shaping the next chapter of privacy-preserving technology. Each keynote speaker brings a unique vantage point on how cryptography, regulation, and user protections intersect.

Keynote Speaker

Hester Peirce

Commissioner, US Securities and Exchange Commission

Commissioner Peirce—widely known as “Crypto Mom”—has been a consistent advocate for thoughtful, innovation-forward regulatory frameworks that help emerging privacy technologies flourish.

Keynote Speaker

Matt Green

Professor of Computer Science, Johns Hopkins University

Professor Green co-founded the Johns Hopkins Information Security Institute and brings deep expertise in applied cryptography, secure messaging, and privacy-preserving protocols.

Keynote Speaker

Neha Narula

Director, Digital Currency Initiative at MIT

As director of MIT’s Digital Currency Initiative, Narula leads interdisciplinary work on digital currency research, privacy, and resilient infrastructure for open financial systems.

Keynote Speaker

Peter Van Valkenburgh

Executive Director, Coin Center

Van Valkenburgh has championed clear policy guardrails for privacy in digital assets, helping lawmakers and the public understand how civil liberties and compliance can coexist.

Keynote Speaker

Wei Dai

Research Partner, 1kx

Dai focuses on advancing zero-knowledge systems and privacy-preserving infrastructure, offering a builder’s view on how cutting-edge research becomes usable tools.

Program note: If the federal government remains shut down on October 16, Commissioner Peirce will be unable to attend the Privacy Summit.

Summit Agenda

What to expect

  • Keynote insights from regulators, builders, and researchers at the front line of privacy innovation.
  • Panel conversations that translate cutting-edge cryptography into practical policy frameworks.
  • A forthcoming detailed timetable with networking breaks, workshops, and side sessions.

Discussion Panel

How to Fight the Lazarus Group

The crypto industry and the government have a common foe. Could they join forces? How?

Discussion Panel

What ZK Can—and Can’t—Do for You

The promise of zero-knowledge cryptography is undeniable. But what specific problems can it address today in digital ID, age verification, privacy, and personal data security? What are its most important limitations?

Discussion Panel

How to Avoid a Dystopian Digital ID System

Governments worldwide are rolling out laws requiring age verification to access content, and the EU is advancing a digital ID wallet that alarms privacy advocates. What can technologists do to keep these tools from undermining personal data security?

Discussion Panel

How Private Can a Stablecoin Be?

The GENIUS Act asked the US Treasury to surface innovative methods for detecting illicit activity in digital assets. What do those methods look like, and what do they mean for user privacy? How private can a compliant stablecoin really be?
